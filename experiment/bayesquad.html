Please read the following instructions very carefully:


In the following study, it is your task to learn about a frequency distribution as well as you can. Learning about a frequency distribution means that you can observe how many people fell into different categories when we asked 1000 people to provide us with their measurements. For example, if we had asked 1000 people about their weekly alcohol consumption, maybe 100 would have said to consume more than 40 units.

Your task will be the following:

1. Provide us with a self-assessment. For example, state how much you drink per week.

2. Estimate how many people will fall below your value. For example, estimate how many people (out of 1000) you think drink less than you.

3. Explore a frequency chart. Here, you will have the chance to explore a frequency distribution. Imagine that we have asked 1000 people about their drinking habits. Now you can check, for example, how many of them drank between 5 and 7 units or between 3 and 5 units, etc. However, you only have 5 queries available. This means you can only look up how many people fell in between a certain range for five different ranges. It is your task to look up these frequencies as wisely as possible in order to assess where on the distribution you would be later on.

4. Re-assess your original assessment. This time gives you the opportunity to reassess your original assessment based on the knowledge you have gained while exploring the frequency graph. For example, once you have gotten an intuition about how many people fell within which range, it should be easier for you to assess your own habits, i.e. how many people drink less than you.
